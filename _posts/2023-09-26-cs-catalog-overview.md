## Motivation

Our primary goal of this project is to facilitate greater understanding and willingness to learn within the field of machine learning for students who may be confused on how to begin.  To this end, our priority is to create a functioning website people will utilize in order to properly gauge certain skills and advancements ahead of time.  Our target audience and focus will be computer science college students.  We can prevent such students from taking on coursework before they pay for a bad experience. In other websites with the same goals, people experience slow progression, because the course isn't best optimized for a clean learning experience.

### Learning Objectives

- Basic knowledge of HTML and JavaScript
    
- Experience on client response
    
- Browse and optimize a larger known database 
    
- Provide free access
    

## Project

We are building what is essentially a collaborative wiki with assisted learning detailing machine learning topics.  It will function to be a well-organized and easy-to-navigate cornerstone of building beginner and intermediate knowledge that can be updated and edited by the community.  With functionality that expands outside of a typical wiki catered for the average student to learn, it will serve as not only an efficient source of knowledge, but a chance to hone one's understanding.

  

Topic Organization: Categorize machine learning topics such as supervised learning, unsupervised learning, deep learning, natural language processing, reinforcement learning, and more.

  

Algorithm Listings: Provide detailed information on machine learning algorithms, including descriptions, use cases, and code examples.

  

Library and Tool References: List popular machine learning libraries and tools, along with installation instructions and tutorials.

  

Resource Recommendations: Alongside information and supplementary knowledge on each article, suggest books, online courses, tutorials, and research papers related to machine learning to further increase user understanding.

## Management

Jason Phan and Giovanni Zamora are the two team leads.  We will co-lead and sync meetings with teams in respect to our sprint timeline.  As such, meetings will be held bi-weekly to plan and check in with the team.

## Timeline

The planned roadmap for this project should neatly result in a functional wiki by the end of the semester.  As with all wikis and databases, they must be constantly maintained to ensure quality as time passes, and as such wikis will still be active after the semester.  

  

Development of the wiki will be split into sprints that tackle different focuses.  Each sprint is planned to be bi-weekly and will consist of each step of the typical software development cycle.  Each sprint will begin with a period of three days in which each member of the team will be contacted for status updates and preparation.

Total understanding and knowledge of the field of Machine Learning will not be required.  Machine learning is vast and complicated, and no one needs to find every bit of information by themselves.  Within this project, we will communicate and collaborate amongst each other to share our knowledge and outreach to professionals and experienced individuals for their input as well.  Strong communication skills and a willingness to outreach is necessary for this project.  Our hope is to learn from this website and our experiences as much as any future user will.

  

| Sprints | Feature                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| A       | Design a prototype of the catalog's user interface and establish initial articles tackling at least five topics.                                                                     |
| B       | The primary focus is to implement users.  Set up user authentication and registration as well as add user reviews/comments and rating features.                                      |
| C       | Develop collaborative editing capabilities across the wiki platform.  Additionally, moderation system and version control would be necessary to implement alongside this.            |
| D       | Implement functionality to increase efficiency and ease of use, such as search and filter functionalities as well as indexes, featured and latest articles, and tags.                |
| E       | Market and promote the wiki to potential users for a successful launch. Encourage user contributions and content creation and establish guidelines for content quality and accuracy. |
