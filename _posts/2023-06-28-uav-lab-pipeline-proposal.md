# Collaborative Proposal: UAV Lab and SEA

Resolving the need for Software-Oriented Students in UAV Lab Projects through collaborative efforts with SEA, a Computer Science Club at California State Polytechnic University, Pomona. Providing advertisement and education initiatives to introduce highly motivated students to the UAV Lab.

June 28, 2023  

## Problem Statement

Searching for proficient CS students to contribute to the UAV lab that can assist in the development and maintenance of software applications. Particularly interested in individuals who demonstrate dedication and a willingness to engage in long-term involvement with the UAV projects.

## Proposed Solution 

Pursuing the development of a comprehensive pipeline that involves the creation of an interview system to identify students who are committed to participating in the projects. This pipeline incorporates the collaboration of the Software Engineering Association (SEA), one of the Computer Science clubs. By integrating the club's resources, a consistent advertising approach can be employed to attract Computer Science majors effectively. Furthermore, SEA may offer workshops and information distribution to educate and train students, enabling them to possess the necessary skills for software-related work in the UAV lab. Notably, this proposal aligns seamlessly with SEA's mission to provide valuable experience to the students of Cal Poly Pomona.

### Why SEA?

Cooperation with the Software Engineering Association (SEA) is beneficial for several reasons. First, the club can provide a consistent stream of capable students who can contribute to maintaining quality control within the UAV Lab. This is achieved through educational initiatives and effective advertisement strategies.

The educational initiatives organized by SEA can greatly enhance the skills of students who are interested in joining the UAV Lab. Workshops focusing on various topics such as data science, data collection, data wrangling, and machine learning modeling can provide a solid foundation for students to excel in UAV projects. These workshops, while treated separately from the pipeline, serve as a preparation platform for students who aspire to be a part of the UAV Lab.

In terms of advertisement, SEA has already established itself within the computer science community. Leveraging this position, the club can effectively promote the interest in joining the UAV Lab to students with a strong software background. By utilizing their existing connections and networks, SEA can attract more software-oriented students who possess the necessary skills and interests to contribute to the UAV Lab.

Overall, partnering with SEA allows for a continuous supply of capable students, ensuring quality control within the UAV Lab. The club's educational initiatives and proficient advertisement strategies contribute to enhancing the skill set of potential candidates and attracting qualified software-focused students to join the UAV Lab.

## Overview of Solution Methodology:

1. Advertisement: Promote the opportunity through club channels, notice boards, and other appropriate platforms. 
    
2. Interviews: Conduct technical interviews administered by a committee. 
    
3. (Optional) Confirmation: Applicants are reviewed and confirmed by Dr. Bhandari, or by a trusted individual/s.
    

### Advertisement:

The chosen methods of advertisement will be tailored based on the preferences and habits of the current student body. It is essential to acknowledge that students may alter their preferred methods for accessing news and information. Therefore, the club e-board will thoroughly evaluate and determine the most effective and suitable channels for advertising, taking into account the evolving preferences of the target audience.

Constant methods for advertisement would include:

- Emails: Sending out targeted emails to relevant student groups, such as Computer Science, Computer Information Systems, and Electrical and Computer Engineering departments. 
    
- Flyers: Creating visually appealing flyers and distributing them strategically in high-traffic areas on campus, such as bulletin boards and common areas. 
    
- Announcements in Club Meetings: Making announcements about the opportunity during the club meetings of the targeted audience, ensuring maximum exposure. 
    
- Announcements in Other CS/CIS/ECE Club Meetings: Collaborating with other related clubs, such as Computer Science, Computer Information Systems, and Electrical and Computer Engineering clubs, to spread the word about the opportunity through their meetings and events.
    

### Interviews

The technical interviews will be organized and conducted by a designated committee, and the process for electing committee members will be discussed later in the proposal. These interviews will focus on assessing skills relevant to image recognition, TensorFlow usage, and other related areas. The content of the technical interviews may be subject to change at the discretion of Dr. Bhandari. UAV software leads would be consulted to determine the total amount of individuals needed to be recruited and what type of skill that each lead would prefer to have in the new recruits. The club would be responsible of providing meaningful methods of providing resources based on the software leads needs.

During the interviews, interviewers will provide reasons for applicants who did not pass. This information will be utilized by the committee to make decisions regarding the next steps for each applicant. For instance, a competent applicant who requires additional time to acquire certain skills may be offered an alternative project hosted by the club, which is separate from Dr. Bhandari's projects. Such cases can be managed by SEA.

The duration of the interviews will depend on the number of applicants. If there is a larger-than-expected pool of applicants, it is recommended to consider conducting 30-minute group interviews. Alternatively, if the number of applicants allows, individual 30-minute interviews can be scheduled. Allocating 2-4 weeks for the interviews is recommended. However, if the volume of applicants extends the interview period beyond 4 weeks (taking into account group interviews), a cutoff based on the application submission date may be implemented. If this solution proves insufficient, the final decision will be at the discretion of the club e-board or Dr. Bhandari. The club e-board will be involved in managing applicants who do not pass the interviews in that scenario.

#### Interview Committee Member Selection:

The selection of committee members will primarily be made by Dr. Bhandari. Alternatively, the current UAV software leads will be responsible for choosing committee members. It is important to note that the UAV software leads themselves may also be part of the committee, but they will be involved in conducting the 2nd round of interviews. If there is a need for additional committee members, it is recommended to develop a rubric for evaluating applicants and provide brief training to trusted individuals who can assist in conducting the interviews.

#### Interview Structure Overview:

Questions are split into two groups: baseline and software lead needs. The baseline questions contain questions to determine a interviewee’s understanding to code and have suboptimal work ethic to ensure the work in each project is properly done. Then the software lead questions is the combination of all the current software leads’ needs to ensure those that fall into a certain project has the capability of contributing to a project’s needs.

### (Optional) Confirmation

In an optional 3rd round, applicants who have successfully passed the previous rounds will have their credentials and information forwarded to Dr. Bhandari. This step allows Dr. Bhandari to have the final decision-making authority and confirm whether the applicants can become part of his UAV projects. The management of these lists will be handled by the interview committee. In the scenario where Dr. Bhandari is unable to confirm applicants, it is recommended to delegate the confirmation role to a trusted individual/s.

## Conclusion

In conclusion, this comprehensive proposal presents a solution to effectively address the need for software-oriented students in UAV Lab Projects. Through the establishment of a collaborative partnership with SEA, a computer science club at California State Polytechnic University, Pomona, the goal is to attract and engage a continuous stream of capable students with the required skills and passion for software development. The proposed methodology, encompassing targeted advertisement, rigorous interview processes, and educational initiatives, ensures the recruitment of talented individuals who will contribute to the success of the UAV Lab Projects. This collaborative effort not only serves as a quality control mechanism but also fosters a mutually beneficial relationship between the UAV Lab and SEA, creating an environment conducive to student growth and project advancement. By implementing this proposal, the capabilities of the UAV Lab will be strengthened, the student experience enhanced, and successful outcomes achieved in the field of unmanned aerial vehicles.
